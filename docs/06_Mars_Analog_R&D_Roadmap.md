# EcoForge Mars Analog R&D Roadmap

**Version**: 1.0 – March 22, 2026

**Purpose**: Strategic phased plan for Mars-relevant ECLSS and bioregenerative experiments in the Mars bay modules. Positions the Premium Hub as a credible terrestrial analog facility for long-duration Mars missions.

## 1. Mission Alignment
- Primary goal: Demonstrate 60–85% practical loop closure under Mars-analog conditions (low pressure 0.3–0.6 atm, elevated CO₂, regolith analogs).  
- Secondary goals: Generate verifiable data, prototypes, and learning for bioregenerative life support.  
- Focus: Short-to-medium duration tests (30–180 days) that are realistic for a small team.

## 2. Phased Timeline

**Phase 1 – Proof-of-Concept (Months 6–18)**  
- Baseline Earth-normal testing of full loop.  
- Experiments: Closed nutrient cycling, biogas CO₂ enrichment, initial low-pressure chamber runs (0.6 atm).  
- Success: Ammonia removal >70%, consistent plant growth.

**Phase 2 – Analog Validation (Months 18–36)**  
- Introduce Mars stressors (low pressure, regolith analogs, thermal cycling).  
- Experiments: 90-day sealed runs, perchlorate mitigation via vermi/biofloc, O₂ generation tests.  
- Success: Water recovery >65%, nutrient cycling >70% under stress.

**Phase 3 – Advanced Integration (Months 36+)**  
- Multi-node cluster testing.  
- Experiments: 180-day runs, prototype kit development, data packaging for grants/corporate partners.  
- Success: Publishable data set and replication templates.

## 3. Key Technologies (Locked from Archive)
- Biofloc hybrid (Bacillus + Nitrosomonas/Nitrobacter + Rhodopseudomonas)  
- Aquaponics with Nile × Blue tilapia  
- Duckweed (Lemna minor) for nitrate scrubbing  
- Vermiponics for solids processing  
- Biogas CO₂ piping  
- Geothermal + solar power

## 4. Monitoring & Data Collection
- Daily: pH, DO, ammonia, nitrate, CO₂ levels  
- Weekly: Biomass yields, water balance  
- Key metrics: Loop closure %, plant growth rates, microbial stability

## 5. Outputs & Value
- Data sets for publications and grants  
- Prototype kits (low-pressure trays, biofloc starters)  
- Potential for NASA/ESA analog collaboration and corporate tech transfer (Tesla/SpaceX ecosystem)
